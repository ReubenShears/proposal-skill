# Optimally — Conversion Ecosystem: Offer Pitch Flow (v2)

**What this is:** the single source of truth for how we pitch the £2,500 Conversion Ecosystem, and the explicit list of what the client gets for their money. Every proposal and the proposal-generating skill inherit their spine and their deliverables list from here. Change the pitch *here first*, then downstream.

**Golden rule:** the page is free; the **result** is paid. We never sell the vehicle — we sell a filled calendar. Everything below makes the paid engine feel categorically better than the free page.

---

## THE FLOW (the sequence, every call)

1. **Talk them through the demo** (screenshare).
2. **Tell them the demo is just a shell.**
3. **Tell them there are 3 gaps** (use the car analogy).
4. **The 3 gaps — Offer, Video, Traffic** — each with how / why / proof.
5. **Show the guarantee.**
6. **Show the model** — ROI on their own numbers, assuming *only* the guarantee is met.
7. **Pitch the price explicitly, and go for the close.**

Everything below is the script and the substance for each step.

## 1–2. The demo, and "it's just a shell"

Walk them through the page you built. Then reframe it before they fall in love with it as-is:

> "Now — everything you're looking at is real, it's built on your brand, and you keep it either way. But be clear: **what you're looking at is a shell.** A page on its own is a **parked car.** It looks the part, but it doesn't move. Three things make it actually drive and put clients on your calendar — and right now the page is missing all three on purpose."

The shell has three visible holes. Point at each on screen — it does the selling for you:

| The page HAS | The page is missing | Filled by |
|---|---|---|
| Layout, structure, your branding | **No VSL** — the video box is empty | **Video** |
| A headline that's our best guess | **No engineered offer** — pages convert on the offer, not the pixels | **Offer** |
| A live URL | **No traffic** — a billboard in the desert | **Traffic** |

Then draw the line that kills the "I'll just take the free page" exit:

> "So the page is yours free. **What you'd pay for is the Offer, the Video, and the Traffic** — plus the guarantee. That's the difference between a nice-looking page and a page that fills your calendar."

## 3–4. The three gaps

Each gap uses the same beats: **What it is → What we do → Why it's the real problem → Why they can't just do it themselves → Proof → The result.** Identical structure across all three — that's what makes it templatable.

### GAP 1 — THE OFFER
*"The page is only as good as the offer on it."*

- **What it is:** the single promise on the page that makes a cold stranger stop and put their hand up — the hook and the deal, not the design.
- **What we do:** a focused ~1-hour session combining *their* domain expertise with *our* pattern library from running dozens of funnels, engineering their service into a **cold-traffic-ready offer** mapped to the four value levers (dream outcome, perceived likelihood, time, effort).
- **Why it's the real problem:** a page converts on its offer, not its pixels. Most wasted ad spend — "impressions, no clicks" — is an **offer problem wearing a traffic costume.** Fixing the offer is usually the single biggest lever on the whole funnel.
- **Why they can't just do it themselves:** the cobbler's-children gap — they're too close to their own business to see how a cold stranger reads it in two seconds. Offer-engineering is pattern recognition earned across many funnels, not something you brainstorm from inside the business.
- **Proof:** their own underperforming ads are proof the offer is the missing lever · `[client opt-in / offer-lift proof to fill]`.
- **The result:** a message cold traffic actually responds to — the line between "impressions, no clicks" and a calendar that fills.

### GAP 2 — THE VIDEO (VSL)
*"A salesman that works while you sleep."*

- **What it is:** the video sales letter embedded in the page that warms every visitor *before* they ever speak to the client.
- **What we do:** we **script it and edit it for them**, done-for-you, on a proven VSL structure (hook → problem → mechanism → proof → CTA). *(Format is flexible — faceless/brand-led or them on camera; we turn their expertise into the script either way.)*
- **Why it's the real problem:** most founders close well *once they're on the phone* — but that warming only happens *after* a booking, and it doesn't scale. The VSL does that warming up front, at scale, so prospects arrive pre-sold. Without it, a cold visitor is staring at a form with no reason to fill it in.
- **Why they can't just do it themselves:** VSL scripting is direct-response craft and the editing (retention pacing, captions) is a production skill. Most founders freeze on camera or ramble for ten minutes. We compress their knowledge into 3–5 minutes that converts.
- **Proof:** Casey runs this exact structure → **~500 leads/mo** at a ~$5–5.5k ticket. The VSL is *why* the funnel converts instead of just collecting clicks.
- **The result:** prospects hit the calendar already three-quarters sold — higher show-up, higher close, less selling to do live. The close rate holds up on **strangers**, not just referrals.

### GAP 3 — THE TRAFFIC
*"A billboard in the desert sells nothing."*

- **What it is:** actual people arriving at the page. This is how we generate the leads.
- **What we do (included, done-for-you):** we build a **reactivation newsletter** from the audience they *already own* — CRM, LinkedIn, email history — filter it to real prospects, and drive them to the page over the 90 days. **This is how we hit the guaranteed calls, and it costs them nothing extra because it's their own audience.**
- **The ads mention (one line, right here):** *"And if you ever want faster results, you can put your own budget behind Meta ads — we'll build and manage the whole thing for you free as a bonus. But you never have to; the newsletter carries the guarantee on its own."*
- **Why it's the real problem:** the Offer, the Video, and the page are inert without eyeballs. Most established businesses are sitting on a dormant goldmine — years of contacts that have gone quiet since referrals slowed.
- **Why they can't just do it themselves:** they *won't* (list-building, segmentation, deliverability, send copy are tedious and specialist) and they *haven't* (it's sat unused for years). Paid ads they've usually already tried alone and burned money on. Traffic that converts is a skill we've already paid the tuition for.
- **Proof:** across client funnels we've moved **~$1.2M** in pipeline · their own dormant list is the asset sitting right there.
- **The result:** the booked calls we guarantee — from an audience that costs nothing extra.

## 5. The Guarantee

- **The build:** full funnel (Offer + Video + Traffic) live in **7 days**; the rest of the 90 days spent driving traffic and booking calls.
- **The guarantee (the only thing we *guarantee*):** **5 booked calls within 90 days, or we keep working for free until you get them.**
- **Why it's safe for us:** we guarantee only what we control — *calls*, never closes or revenue.
- **The line:** *"If I didn't think you could get the 5 calls, I'd have turned you away. I'll personally make sure it happens — even if I have to phone the leads myself."*

---

## 6. The Model — ROI on their numbers (assume ONLY the guarantee is met)

**The rule:** take *their* stated close rate and cut it well below their own expectation — as high as stays credible, but under their number — then use their **average ticket**. Illustrated output must clear the fee comfortably. **Frame it as an illustration, never a promise** ("here's what this looks like on your numbers") — only the 5 calls are guaranteed. This is the specific fix for the "you promised the earth" objection.

**The formula:**
`5 guaranteed calls × [conservative close rate] × [their average ticket] = illustrated floor revenue` → compare to the ~£2,500 fee.

**Illustrative example** *(numbers vary per prospect — this is just to show the shape):* a prospect who closes ~85% and has a ~$3,500 average ticket → sandbag to 60% → 5 × 60% = 3 clients × $3,500 = **$10,500 off the guaranteed floor vs a ~$3,000 fee ≈ 3.5×** — *"and that's the minimum, at a close rate well below your own."*

## 7. Price & Close

- **Price, stated plainly:** **£2,500 (~$3,000)** for the full 90-day engagement — roughly **£1k/month** — positioned as the intro offer built to earn the long-term partnership.
- **Payment:** upfront by default; a **monthly split** is the one concession to trade for a decision on the call. *(A "can I split it?" question is a buying signal, not an objection — lean in.)*
- **The close:** trial close + tie-down — *"Assuming the page looks right, is there anything between us and getting started?"* Isolate the real blocker, then ask for the decision on the call.
